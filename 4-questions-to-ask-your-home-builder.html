4 Questions to Ask Your Home Builder

July 27, 2023

Building a new home is a great way to obtain the ideal home for you and your family. According to Statista, in the United States, the majority of housing units are single-family houses. No matter where you live in the country, if you're planning new-home construction, here are four questions that you should ask a potential home builder.


1. Do You Have References and Work Samples?

A contractor should have lots of experience building homes, and that experience should include references from satisfied customers. A home builder should also have photographic evidence of homes the contractor has built. You should ask for references and work samples. You also could learn the locations of some you could drive to and observe in person. You likely won't be able to tour a home, but you can get a look at its exterior and general build quality.


2. Are You Bonded and Insured?

Much could go wrong while a home construction project is underway. When something goes wrong, the costs go up. In worst-case scenarios, entire projects are destroyed. A fire, improper construction, and many other potential mishaps might completely destroy the home and make it impossible to continue. You need to know your contractor is bonded and insured so you can feel confident your new-home project and your investment in it are protected.


3. Who Obtains the Construction Permits?

A home builder needs to have the necessary building permits for construction to occur. Without them, the entire project is jeopardized and might be ruled illegal by local authorities. You should make sure you know who's obtaining the construction permits so everything is in place to enable the lawful construction of your new home.


4. How Long Would Construction Take?

You might have a particular timeline in mind for your new home to be built. If the home builder cannot complete the project during that timeline, you need to either modify your expectations or find another contractor who can do the work in the amount of time you would expect. If the project simply cannot be done as quickly as you would like, the contractor should be able to give you a good reason why.
