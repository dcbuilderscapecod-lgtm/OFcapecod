The 10-Minute Mid-Summer Deck Check

August is when your deck works hardest — cookouts, wet bathing suits, sandy feet, full-sun afternoons. It's also the easiest time to spot trouble, because problems that hide in winter show themselves in dry weather. Here's the ten-minute walk-around we'd do on our own decks this weekend.

Four spots, ten minutes

1. Fasteners. Walk the whole deck and grab the railing every few feet. Salt air corrodes hardware from the inside out, so look for rust streaks bleeding down from joist hangers and rail-post bolts — the streak shows up before the failure does. A screw that spins without tightening means the wood around it has gone soft; move it an inch and drive a new one.

2. The ledger. That's the board bolting the deck to the house, and it's where most deck collapses start. From below, you want to see staggered bolts or structural screws — not just nails. Any gap opening between ledger and house wall is a call-a-pro item.

3. Flashing. Metal flashing should tuck up behind the siding and lap over the top of the ledger, steering rainwater away from the house framing. If you see black staining or peeling paint on the siding right above the deck, water is getting behind it.

4. Rot. Take a screwdriver and press the end grain — post bases, stair stringers, board ends. Sound wood pushes back. If the tip sinks in a quarter inch with light pressure, that member is rotting from the inside.

Repair or rebuild?

Simple rule: if the frame is sound, repair. Deck boards, railings, and stairs can all be replaced piece by piece. But rot in the ledger, posts, or joists means the structure itself is compromised — patching the surface just hides it. Not sure which you're looking at? From the Field
✓ Rust streaks below a joist hanger appear before the hanger fails — check the shadows, not just the hardware.
✓ A railing should take a hard shove without moving; any wobble means a loose or corroded post connection.
✓ Probe wood where water sits — post bases and board ends rot first, long before the surface looks bad.
