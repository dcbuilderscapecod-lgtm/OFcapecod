Thinking about a fall project? Here's how a free consultation actually works

August is when fall projects get decided. Crews book up fast once the summer crowds leave, and any job that needs open walls or a new roof wants to be buttoned up before the November wind. If you've been circling an idea all summer, a free consultation is how it stops being an idea.

The consultation, step by step

Call (508) 566-1190 or use request a consultation and we set a time that works. Dave Cleary — owner, 40-plus years building on the Cape — does the walkthrough himself. He looks at the things that decide a Cape Cod project: framing and structure, moisture paths, insulation and air sealing, and how new work ties into your existing roof and siding. Then he asks what you want the space to do, not just how you want it to look.

You'll have a written estimate within 24 hours. No fee, no obligation — the estimate is yours to sit with. If we do the job, Dave is on every job site alongside foreman Spencer Cleary, so the person who scoped the work is the person watching it get built.

How to prepare your wish list

Fifteen minutes of prep makes the consult twice as useful:

  • Rank it. Split the list into must-have, nice-to-have, and someday — it shows us where the flexibility lives.
  • Note the annoyances. Cold rooms, drafts, a bath fan that does nothing — symptoms point straight to fixes.
  • Decide what energy efficiency means to you. Lower winter bills? A house that holds its temperature? Less oil or propane? We build high-performance and energy-smart, and your answer shapes the spec.
  • Gather photos and rough sketches. They anchor the conversation; nothing needs to be pretty.
