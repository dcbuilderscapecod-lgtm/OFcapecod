Get Your Heat Ready Before the First Cold Night

The first real cold snap on the Cape usually lands in October, and that's the worst possible day to discover your heat won't fire. Every item on this list takes minutes now and saves you a shivering, expensive scramble later. Run through it this week while the weather's still forgiving.

The checklist, in order

  • Book a professional tune-up now. HVAC techs are wide open in early fall and slammed by November. A tune-up cleans the burner or heat exchanger, checks combustion, and catches worn parts before they strand you.
  • Test the system this week. Set the thermostat 5 degrees above room temperature and let it run 15 minutes. Listen for banging, smell for burning dust past the first few minutes, and confirm every room warms up.
  • Bleed hot-water radiators and baseboards. Trapped air makes the top of a radiator stay cold while the bottom heats. Open the bleed valve until water sputters out, then close it. Start with the highest floor.
  • Change the filter, then set a schedule. A clogged filter forces the blower to work harder and starves rooms of airflow. Check 1-inch filters monthly during heating season; thicker media filters last longer, so follow the manufacturer's interval.
  • Test CO detectors and check the date. Any fuel-burning system can produce carbon monoxide. Press the test button, replace batteries, and replace any unit past its printed expiration, usually 7 to 10 years.

Then ask the bigger question

A tuned furnace still can't outrun a leaky house. Where does your home lose heat, drafty windows, a cold bonus room, thin attic insulation? If the fix turns out to be a building problem rather than a mechanical one, that's the work we do, and our blog covers more of it.

From the Field
✓ Vacuum baseboard fins before the season starts; crushed or dusty fins choke heat output.
✓ Keep furniture and curtains at least a few inches off radiators and registers so air can actually circulate.
✓ Write the install date on every new filter with a marker so the schedule keeps itself.
