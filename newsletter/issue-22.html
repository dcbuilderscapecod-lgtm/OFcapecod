The Hardest-Working Room in the House

December showers mean foggy mirrors, cold tile, and a fan that runs while heated air disappears outside. The bathroom is the smallest room in the house and one of the hardest working. This week: how a remodel can manage moisture and heat at the same time, and what that means for you.

Get the fan right first

An exhaust fan is moisture control and an energy detail in one. The common sizing guideline is one CFM per square foot of floor, with a 50 CFM minimum — more for a big soaking tub or a separate shower room. Runtime matters as much as size: steam keeps rising off wet surfaces after you towel off, so the fan should run about 20 minutes after a shower. A timer switch or humidity-sensing control handles that without anyone remembering. And check the duct — it should run short, smooth, and insulated to the outdoors, never dumped into the attic, where it feeds mold and rots sheathing.

Heat where you feel it, keep it where it belongs

  • Heated floors. Electric mats under tile warm the surface you actually touch, which lets many people keep the room thermostat lower and still feel comfortable.
  • Glazing. A single-pane bathroom window sweats all winter. A low-e, insulated replacement stops the condensation and the draft in one move.
  • Waterproofing. Modern shower membranes do double duty: they keep water out of the wall cavity — and a wet wall cavity is an insulation failure waiting to happen.
  • Fixtures. Every gallon of hot water you don't use is a gallon your water heater never had to heat. WaterSense showerheads have come a long way.

Efficiency means something different in every house — lower bills, warmer feet, a window that finally stays clear. If you're weighing a bath update, our remodeling services page covers the full scope, and a free consultation is a good way to sort your priorities.

From the Field
✓ If the mirror is still fogged five minutes after a shower, the fan is undersized, ducted badly, or both.
✓ Pick a fan rated quiet enough to actually run — a loud fan gets switched off and stays off.
✓ Put heated floors on a programmable thermostat so they warm before your morning shower, not all day long.
