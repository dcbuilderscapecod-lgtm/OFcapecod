The efficient holiday home: lights, loads, and a full house

This week your house works harder than it does all year — lights on every eave, the oven running for hours, guest rooms heated, doors opening all day. Here's where that energy actually goes, and a few easy calls that keep the bills sane and the house safe through New Year's.

The lights: cheap to run, if they're LED

Old incandescent strings turn most of their power into heat — that's why they're warm to the touch and why you can't chain many together. LED strings run cool, draw a small fraction of the power, and last far longer. If a set feels warm, that's your sign to replace it. Put outdoor lights on a timer: dusk to midnight covers every visitor without you thinking about it.

Loads you can't see

Phantom loads are devices drawing power while "off" — cable boxes, game consoles, chargers, anything with a clock or standby light. December adds more: inflatables, extra TVs, plug-in decorations. A switched power strip kills them all in one flip. Walk the house one evening with the lights off; every glowing dot is a draw that never quits.

Comfort with a full house

  • Thermostat: people are little furnaces. Hosting a crowd with the oven going? Set it a degree or two lower than usual — the room lands right where you want it.
  • Space heaters: straight into a wall outlet, never a power strip or extension cord; three feet of clearance from bedding, curtains, and tree skirts; off when you leave the room or go to sleep.
  • Guest rooms: open closed-off rooms a day early, and make sure vents and baseboards aren't blocked by luggage or an air mattress.
