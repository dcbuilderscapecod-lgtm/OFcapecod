Planning a 2027 project? Start here.

Happy New Year from all of us at DC Builders. If a remodel or addition is on your 2027 list, these quiet January weeks are the time to plan it. Decisions changed on paper cost nothing; the same decisions changed mid-construction cost money, time, and patience. Here is the order that works.

Scope first, budget second

Write down what the project has to do before anyone talks numbers: another bedroom, a kitchen that seats six, a first-floor bath, a warmer family room. A clear scope lets a builder price the same thing every time — a vague one guarantees estimates you can't compare. What drives cost, in plain terms: moving plumbing, opening structural walls, adding foundation, changing the roofline, and the finish level you choose. Keeping the footprint and the wet walls where they are is the single biggest cost-tamer available to you.

Permits, timelines, and the energy decision

Every Cape town — Falmouth, Mashpee, Bourne included — runs its own building department, and lots near wetlands or the coast often add conservation review. None of it is scary, but it takes weeks on the calendar, so a realistic project runs design, then permitting, then construction. Plan in months, and you'll never feel rushed into a bad decision.

Here's the part most people skip: energy performance is a planning-stage decision. Insulation depth, air-sealing details, window specs, and heat pump sizing all get set on paper, where changing them costs a pencil stroke. After drywall, changing them costs demolition. So ask yourself what efficiency means to you — lower bills, rooms that hold an even temperature, quieter walls, a house that rides out a winter outage? Your answer should shape the plan.
